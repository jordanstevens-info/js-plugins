# Front Gate Tickets Glossary of Terms

Source: Front Gate Tickets Glossary of Terms | Last Updated December 2018

This glossary contains the most frequently used and referenced terms at Front Gate Tickets. It is not an exhaustive list.

---

## Definitions

### A

**3D Secure** — A payment tool Front Gate's system can turn on at the label level to add more security for online transactions. Technically, it is a standard created by Visa and MasterCard to further secure CNP (Card Not Present) transactions over the Internet. It is an added authorization step in the online purchase experience. Front Gate's Fraud Department is responsible for turning on or off 3D Secure in Backstage.

**A/B Testing** — A/B testing is a methodology FGT employs to present two different versions of the website or a specific feature on a webpage to a user at random. The intent is to determine which variation (A or B) performs better for a given hypothesis by analyzing results of the test. EG Product could conclude that when users see webpage version A, they are more inclined to buy the collectable credential on purchase path.

**AC** — Access Control is what enables the "in's and out's" onsite at an event. This term is often used in the phrase AC Site. See Access Control Site.

**Access Control Site** — An access control site (or AC Site) is configured in Backstage. It is how the on-site structure of an event is configured so that tickets can be associated with where and when it can be used at an event. It is also where the devices that will scan at an event are configured and managed. For instance, if there is a VIP lounge inside a festival, our access control site configuration will make sure that only attendees that have a VIP wristband are scanned in green for that lounge.

**Access Group** — An Access Group is part of an Access Control Site that allows you to set rules specific to an area on site. Rules that can be set at the Access Group level include start times, in/out frequency limits, and setting which kinds of tickets or price types can scan green.

**Account** — When we refer to a customer account, we mean a Front Gate Tickets account. Historically we would give the option for consumers to check out online as a guest as an alternative to using an account. Starting in 2018, we eliminated that option. Now all users who purchase a ticket on *.frontgatetickets.com have an account with FGT. Since the FGT account system stores the customer's information, we enable a quicker checkout experience after login by prepopulating their address, phone number, stored card if they have one, etc. It also powers online self-service functions on orders.

**ACL** — ACL is the acronym for one of our largest festivals, Austin City Limits Music Festival. It is promoted by C3 Presents and happens in Front Gate's home town of Austin.

**Activation** — Activation is a term used in Marketing and Sponsorship. "Brand Activation" is way of driving Fan action through brand interaction and experiences. In simple terms, the key aim of these sorts of campaigns is to get consumers to act, hence the use of the term "activation". A booth inside a festival where an attendee can participate in a game and win prizes like a koozie with a State Farm logo on it is a type of activation. Brands spend money to sponsor these types of activations making our Sponsorship team one of our revenue drivers at Front Gate.

**Add Ons** — Sometimes used to refer to Upsells in the online purchase process, "add on" is a term also used in AdmitOne for adding a new level of access (via an access-group) to a wristband, usually on-site at a festival. For this feature to be available in AdmitOne, specific Access Control configuration steps must happen in Backstage.

**Admit One (TAK)** — Admit One used to be called TAK, which is an acronym for Ticketing Army Knife. It is one of our Big Three along with Purchase Path and Backstage. Admit One is our box office sales and ticket help platform. It is used by Customer Service Reps for phone sales and is also used for fulfillment and printing tickets on site and in our own FGT warehouse. From a technical perspective, it is the Angular front end for the box office system.

**Akamai** — Akamai is a technology vendor that we use only during HVOs for load balancing and wait room load management.

**Alternate Email** — The alternate email you will see in Backstage Order Details is the email a customer can provide specifically for sending digital tickets. The main email that is used for receipts is by default also the email that will receive any digital ticket unless there is an alternate email specified. Non-digital ticket emails are always sent to the main email address on an order whether an alternate email has been provided or not.

**API** — An application program interface (API) is a set of routines, protocols, and tools for building software applications. Basically, an API specifies how software components should interact. Additionally, APIs are used when programming graphical user interface (GUI) components. See Open API to learn more about FGT APIs.

**Appetize** — Appetize is a partner that provides on-site sales processing hardware and software (POS system) for merchants inside festivals and other live events. FGT partners with vendors like Appetize so that our Cashless wristbands can be used for purchases through their system. A company like Appetize is hired by the festival promoter but requires a technical integration with FGT.

**Archive** — Backstage offers the ability to archive a label. If a label is archived, then its name is appended with "[ARCHIVED]" in any list. You can choose to hide all archived labels via your Account Settings in Backstage. Archiving labels must be done manually.

**Assign (wristband)** — When you associate a wristband to an order, you are assigning that physical piece of inventory to that order. Assigning is part of the fulfillment process. When inventory is assigned, it is also referred to as attached. To unassign, the FGT system more often uses the term "detach".

**Atlassian** — An Australian company that provides many of the cloud-based tools used in our Product Development department including Jira, Service Desk, Confluence and Trello.

**Attendee Capture** — Attendee Capture is a feature that allows ticket purchasers to add personal information to each ticket via the Purchase Path's Order Details page and during a purchase. This was created to comply with a legal/regulatory requirement in the Australian markets. It must be toggled on at the License level in Backstage.

**atVenu** — atVenu is a partner that provides on-site sales processing software (POS system). FGT partners with vendors like atVenu so that our Cashless wristbands can be used for purchases through their system. A company like atVenu is hired by the festival promoter but requires a technical integration with FGT.

**AWS** — AWS stands for Amazon Web Services and is the vendor we use to host all of our system infrastructure.

### B

**Backstage** — Backstage is a Front Gate Tickets application that handles setting up events, labels, licenses and other information management. One of our Big Three along with Purchase Path and Admit One, it is often referred to as our ticketing motherboard. This platform, depending on the user's permission, enables access to ticket purchaser information and ticket build data. Front Gate Operations staff uses this software to build out labels & events, access control and fulfillment configurations, pull ticketing reports, and much more. It is also used by Accounting staff, Marketing, Customer Service staff, and many other parts of a client's or Front Gate Ticket's organization.

**Base** — Base ticket price is the value of a ticket without any fees. Also referred to as the ticket's face value. This is the price typically advertised to the customer.

**BestRing** — BestRing is a partner that provides on-site sales processing hardware and software (POS system) for use by merchants inside festivals. FGT partners with vendors like BestRing so that our Cashless wristbands can be used for purchases through their system. A company like BestRing is hired by the festival promoter but requires a technical integration with FGT.

**Boca Printer** — Boca is the brand name of the thermal printers that FGT and many clients use to print hard tickets. FGT utilizes Boca Lemur-C printers to be exact. Boca's are utilized by both the box office and our fulfillment staff.

**Box Office** — A box office is usually a physical location for selling things on the FGT platform. It can also refer to one of the availability options when configuring where a price type can be sold.

**Braintree** — Braintree is a PayPal company that Front Gate uses to process our credit card payments. We refer to them as a processor. Through our integration with Braintree, we also can offer other payment methods like PayPal and Venmo.

**Build** — A build refers to the configuration of either an event or a label. Backstage is the tool used for configuring all builds.

**Bulk Add** — Bulk Add is a tool used to add a common item to a group of orders at once.

**Bundles/Bundling** — On the purchase path, events can be suggested as a result of carting other events (think, "you bought this, so you might like this..."). Bundles are the groupings of suggestions that pop up in a modal after carting something in our ticketing purchase process. Also referred to as "Upsells", the items are represented visually by their product listing image and can appear in either an upsell modal after a fan has put something in their cart or in a list of recommendations on the cart page.

**Buy Process** — Buy Process is one of the terms used by our engineering team for our Purchase Path or what powers our online ticket sales. See Purchase Path. Buy Process refers to the applicable backend code.

### C

**C3 Presents** — C3 Presents is a promoter of live entertainment and is owned by Live Nation. C3 is one of Front Gate's largest festival clients and has offices in the two floors above FGT's corporate offices in Austin. Front Gate partners with C3 on their North American music festivals including two of the biggest: ACL and Lollapalooza.

**Captcha** — Captcha is a feature that can be enabled at the Label level. It adds a modal with an "I am not a robot" step in the purchase path right before adding tickets to the cart as well as part of the registration process over the web. Captcha is enabled by default on all labels.

**Cardinal Commerce** — Cardinal Commerce is the Braintree vendor for 3D Secure that FGT utilizes. For more information, see 3D Secure.

**Carting** — Carting is a term we use for when an action in an application adds tickets to the shopping cart. When a ticket is carted, it is no longer available for sale although it is not actually sold yet. When a ticket is left abandoned in the cart, we have processes to kick those tickets out of the cart so that they can once again be available for sale.

**Cashless** — With the Cashless add-on component to Registration, a Fan can elect to have their payment information associated with their wristband. This powers "cashless" payments using that registered wristband to pay for food, drink or merchandise at participating merchants inside a festival. Note: What is associated with the wristband in our system is a token, not the actual card data.

**CB** — You will most often see CB in reference to our efforts to combat Fraud. CB is short for chargeback. See Chargeback.

**CC Fee** — CC Fee stands for Credit Card Fee and is a %-based field configured in Backstage and determined via client contract that is also automatically included in the price model. This calculates based on the total transaction.

**Chargeback** — A chargeback is a negative transaction initiated by a FAN through their bank that returns money to the purchaser's credit card usually because the cardholder believes it is fraud. This type of transaction shows up as a refund category in some processes and reports. Our Fraud Department is responsible for "fighting" or trying to reverse chargebacks.

**Close the Day** — Close the Day is an FGT Product Development project name. Close the Day's intent is to make our reporting suite historically accurate. Before 10/2018, for example, the reporting system would attribute post-purchase refunds to the original purchase date rather than the actual refund date, skewing the original onsale data by decreasing revenue and ticket count. Close the Day is the effort to mitigate such behavior.

**CNP** — Card Not Present - CNP is a type of payment card transaction that originates without having a machine physically read a mag stripe or chip on the card. For instance, all our online sales are categorized as CNP.

**Collectible Credential** — A Collectible Credential is a type of merchandise that clients can sell on Front Gate Tickets' platform and is customized for an event. It is promoted as a festival keepsake and helps to drive ancillary revenue.

**COM** — Client Operations Manager - A COM is the day to day contact for our ticketing clients. They act as the account manager sharing best practices, training, answering questions and coordinating the overall success of the festivals they manage. They travel a significant amount going on the road for the festivals they manage and serving as an FGT subject matter expert.

**Commerce API** — The Commerce API is a suite of API endpoints that allow third party partners to create FGT orders outside of the FGT core platform. The Commerce API is a part of the Open API initiative.

**Comp** — Short for "complimentary", a comp is a price type. Comps can be programmed in our system as price types with the code of "C". Some internal users or clients may also utilize $0.00 buy style price types as "comps".

**Confirmed Seat** — In our system, a seat or ticket is moved to a status of "Confirmed" once it is sold. To "re-confirm" a seat in Backstage is to move a seat to a confirmed status.

**Confluence** — A cloud-based tool used by our Product Development Department to document many of the products, initiatives and reference materials pertinent to Product, Engineering and Design at Front Gate.

**COR** — Client Operations Representative - A COR acts as support for the Client Operations Managers. They assist as needed to help manage workload. They are also the primary contact for our venue clients and those festivals for which we do not need to travel. CORs will travel in a support role but do not lead their own festivals on site.

**CSR** — Customer Service Representative - In Front Gate's past, we staffed our own Customer Support Call Center. Now, we outsource this to a Ticketmaster division but still must provide the software and functions that these CSR need to do their job. We also have at least two full time employees devoted to this relationship.

**Custom Barcoding** — Custom Barcoding is a term that refers to a third-party barcode that is tied to a ticket in the FGT system during fulfillment process. Otherwise, each ticket sold through Front Gate is assigned a barcode generated by our system.

### D

**Deploy** — The term "deploy" is used in a couple of different ways at Front Gate. In engineering terms, it means that code is moved to an environment, usually production. To deploy a feature or a bug fix usually means it has gone live in production. Deploy can be used in Field Operations to refer to equipment that is set up and used ("deployed") at an event.

**Detach** — Detach is a term used to refer to inventory (usually in the form of wristbands) that is unassociated with an order so that the inventory in question could be reused on a different order. Associating or attaching is done only through one of our systems.

**Digital Delivery** — Digital Delivery is our feature that enables sending and receiving tickets via email.

**Digital Print Pass** — Digital Print Pass is a shipping type. When using this shipping type, the Fan will receive a secondary email with their ticket attached as a PDF. It is one of our types of digital delivery tickets. One email is sent per ticket purchased.

**Discovery API** — Discovery API is a suite of FGT API endpoints that allow third party partners to view what price types per Access Control Site they are able to perform actions on. It is part of our Open API.

**DPP** — Digital Print Pass - See Digital Print Pass.

### E

**EDM** — Electronic Dance Music - EDM is a music genre that inspires many music festivals.

**EMV** — Europay, Mastercard and Visa - EMV is a global standard for payment cards equipped with computer chips and refers to the technology used to authenticate chip-card transactions.

**EOM** — Employee of the Month! - Every month the executives at Front Gate nominate and then vote on who has gone above and beyond in the month before. The winner is declared Employee of the Month at an All Staff meeting, gets a bonus and an awesome certificate.

**Event** — Events in the Front Gate system are used to group specific items that a Festival wants to sell. Day passes, single day passes, and camping are all types of items that could be grouped in an event. A Festival may be comprised of numerous events. Each event will be built within a specific label. Events are created based on the configuration requested by the Festival, optimized using best practices to achieve the greatest customer experience. Events contain all price types related to a specific offering. Orders will display label and the events purchased.

**Everfest** — A third party commerce partner, Everfest is engaged by some of our clients as an additional platform to sell ticket inventory for their festival. For tickets that are sold on their platform to work onsite at the festival with FGT's access control, their orders have to be imported into FGT's system.

**Exchanges** — An exchange is a method on a completed FGT order to return at least 1 item and simultaneously use the funds to purchase a different item. Exchanges can only happen for tickets within the same festival or event.

**External Method of Payment (MOP)** — External Methods of Payment (or MOPs) are available payment types that can be customized at both the License and Label level. Revenue generated from these payment types is recorded in our system, but Accounting can report on these separately since funds were collected outside the FGT credit card payment system.

### F

**Fan** — Fan is the preferred way for all LNE companies to refer to event attendees and ticket buyers.

**Fastly** — Fastly is a technology vendor that we use for network load balancing and management of our SSL certificates.

**FEST** — FEST (acronym for Fan Engagement Suite) allows Festival Clients to connect with fans via RFID enabled activations and games on-site while seamlessly capturing fan data. FEST products ask fans to engage with brands who are also Sponsors of a festival using RFID wristbands. The original FEST features were created completely in-house. FGT also provides API integrations for additional 3rd party FEST features that our Marketing and Business Development teams can include in our suite of FEST offerings to our clients.

**Festival** — Festival can refer to a couple of different things. Usually it is the umbrella term to describe a group of events happening over the course of one or more days that is owned and/or organized by a Promoter. Festival can also be use synonymously with the definition of client. E.g. you might hear someone saying the Festival requests that builds or changes be done to events. In the Front Gate system, a festival is comprised of events with price types and usually has a Fulfillment Center and an Access Control Site configured in Backstage. Some festivals require more than one label to organize their Festival's events.

**Fevo** — A third party commerce partner, Fevo is engaged by some partners as an additional platform to sell ticket inventory for their festival. For tickets that are sold on their platform to work onsite at the festival with FGT's access control, their orders have to be imported into FGT's system.

**FGT** — Front Gate Tickets - That's US! Our full business name is Front Gate Ticketing Solutions, LLC.

**Fliptix** — Fliptix is a relatively new specialized resale vendor that integrates with FGT APIs. They specialize in reselling unused ticket time during an event. One such use case would allow a Fan to resell their 3-Day wristband on the Fliptix platform after the first day of the festival so that someone else could use their wristband for the remaining two days. Whether or not a festival will have this integration enabled between Fliptix and FGT depends on the festival promoter having an agreement with Fliptix.

**FOM** — Field Operations Manager - A FOM is the site lead for FGT. They are responsible for the planning, installation, and execution of FGT services and technologies. They work in collaboration with the client and FGT COM to coordinate gear needs, logistics, and on-site deliverables to ensure the success of our access control operation.

**Front End** — When you hear someone refer to "the Front End" at Front Gate, they are talking about the UI or User Interface of one of our applications. The "back end" is the code that powers what a user sees on the Front End. The styling of how buttons are shaped, what colors appear in banners, etc. are all aspects of the Front End whereas the logic that determines what happens once an action button is used lives in the Back End. Most often, Front End is used in relation to the Purchase Path.

**Fulfillment** — Fulfillment is one of the departments at FGT. It is also used as a verb and refers to the act of delivering whatever form of ticket was sold to the consumer to the consumer. EG when a digital ticket is emailed to a customer, it is then marked as "fulfilled". Our Fulfillment team works out of our Austin warehouse location and handles hundreds of thousands of items and shipments a year.

**Fulfillment Center** — A fulfillment center is configured in Backstage and is created fresh for each event or festival. It is used by the Fulfillment team to manage and fulfill the inventory of items that must be physically shipped to a customer or prepared for Will Call pick up.

### G

**GA** — General Admission - It is one of the most commonly sold price types in our system.

**Gate** — A gate usually refers to a type of hardware at an entrance to an event that is required for RFID enabled events. Sometimes we also deploy gates to help limit entrance to special areas inside a Festival like a VIP area. FGT gates are designed for Front Gate and consist of a "lid" that holds the technical hardware for scanning RFID wristbands as well as an optional stand. The gates with stands can have special skins on them customized for a festival. Some gates are simply lids that are mounted on a wall. Not all events use gates.

**Gateway** — We use the term Gateway to refer to the company/account facilitating credit card transactions. They are usually associated with either a specific payment processor or client or combination of both.

**Google Analytics** — Google Analytics is a free Web analytics service that Front Gate uses in our Purchase Path. It provides statistics and basic analytical tools for search engine optimization (SEO) and marketing purposes. Google Analytics features include data visualization tools including a dashboard that displays changes in data over time, segmentation for analysis of subsets such as conversions, marketing attribution, custom reports, email-based sharing and communication, and integration with other Google products such as AdWords. One use at Front Gate for Google Analytics is the ability to see how many customers are at different places in the Purchase Path during an onsale.

**Google Tag Manager (GTM)** — Google Tag Manager is a tag management system that allows Marketing to quickly and easily update tracking codes and related code fragments collectively known as "tags" on the Front Gate Tickets websites. Switching from manual pixels to using GTM freed Marketing from having to involve a developer every time they or a client needed to make changes. They can now use as web-based user interface to set up tracking tags, establish triggers that cause a tag to fire when certain events occur, and create variables. Google Tag Manager replaces all manually-coded tags on a site or app, including tags from Google Ads, Google Analytics, Floodlight, and 3rd party tags.

**Groupon** — Groupon is one of Front Gate's partners for selling tickets "off platform". For a client to take advantage of the reach of Groupon in order to sell tickets, a separate label configuration is required in Backstage. Orders placed through Groupon are then imported into the FGT system. Partnerships with companies like Groupon help Front Gate clients reach potential buyers that they do not normally have access to.

**GTM** — Google Tag Manager. See Google Tag Manager.

**Guest List** — Guest Lists are a feature in Backstage at the Event level and is typically utilized for venues only. This is a very lo-fi tool that allows a user to upload a .csv file of names. Then the box office staff can use Backstage to mark these people as having picked up their tickets, but no orders are created. PlusOne is another way to manage a guest list but is very different from the Backstage tool in that it has a customer facing portal, allows for charges to be completed, sends out a variety of emails depending on the type and status of request and creates an order that can be fulfilled like a normal purchase. See PlusOne.

### H

**Handling Fee** — A handling fee is a per ticket or per order fee programmed in Backstage and applied on top of base/service/shipping fees. It can be a flat or percentage fee. For example, Layaway Plan fee is configured as handling. Additional fees can be added at the client's discretion.

**Holds** — Ticket inventory may be placed on hold. This means those tickets are held back from the sellable capacity. This is available for GA and Reserved Events. Holds maybe be placed during the event build process, or after an event has been built. Also, in Backstage, the user can create multiple named holds, as well as restrict selling privileges to staff members. For example, a festival promoter may want to reserve 100 tickets for distributing to special customers and call these their VIP Hold.

**HVO** — High-Volume Onsale – When the business expects an onsale to flood our system with tens of thousands of users at one time we refer to that as an HVO. It is the reason we came up with a set up called a Wait Room. An HVO with a Wait Room is very labor intensive for our Technology, Design and Operations teams to implement so is only used in very specific situations. Most onsales create a load that our system can easily handle without any special setup even with thousands and thousands of users on our webpages.

### I

**Impact Radius** — Impact Radius is a platform that allows us to consolidate our affiliate partnerships into one central location. It streamlines the process of recruiting, contracting, tracking, and paying all partners. Impact Radius powers the Ticketmaster Affiliate Network and FGT Marketing oversees the Front Gate implementation of the platform on our properties.

**Import Orders** — In some cases, not all tickets to a client's event are sold via the Front Gate system. In today's marketplace, clients more and more want to be able to sell tickets through many types of partnerships. There are also times when we are hired simply because of our expertise on site and our RFID access control solution. In those cases, we do not sell the tickets but we do have to know about them. When an order is placed outside the FGT system but needs to be fulfilled by us or needs to work with our Access Control system on-site, we must import the data for those orders into our system. These orders are differentiated in our system by referring to them as "imported" orders.

**Insomniac Presents** — Insomniac Presents is a promoter owned by Live Nation and one of Front Gate's largest festival clients. Insomniac specialized in EDM or Electronic Dance Music events.

**Invalidate** — There are times when a ticket needs to be invalidated. In that case, the barcode of the item can no longer be used and will not be allowed to scan. If the event is RFID, then the invalidated credential will not be able to be re-used.

**Issue** — Issue in AdmitOne refers to a delivery method. If you are onsite and selling a ticket at the Box Office, you are handing the ticket or wristband to the customer in person. In this in-person case, "Issue" is the delivery type.

### J

**Jira** — A cloud-based tool used in our Product Development Department to manage digital tracking of software development tasks and projects. All employees in Product Development automatically get an account to use Jira as it is key to managing change control in that department.

### K

**KID One** — Our KID One application can be used at RFID festivals and creates a reliable and easy way to reunite lost kids with their parents. Because the application requires users to register their information, KID One also gathers powerful data and insights into a festival's fan base. It is a type of registration that links the parents' contact information to the data on the child's wristband. KID One was originally called Tag-A-Kid when it was exclusive to one client, C3 Presents. When we broadened the offering, we changed its name.

**Kiosk** — Kiosk is defined as a small structure used for providing information or displaying advertisements, often incorporating an interactive display screen or screens. Front Gate utilizes specially designed kiosks inside Festivals to allow the Fan to physically interact with FEST software using their wristbands. Clients pay a premium for the use of FGT kiosks and FEST inside their festivals.

**Kount** — Kount is a third-party service provided via the Braintree system to help fight fraudulent orders. Kount is a rule-based system which allows our Fraud team to create triggers that flag orders for potential fraud. It can also automate the rejection of these flagged orders by implementing certain criteria they must pass based on rules.

### L

**Label** — A Label can be defined as the master level control over all the events that make up a festival. Each label will have its own unique URL in our purchase path. The label will contain the constraints that apply to all items related to that specific festival. A festival may be made up of multiple labels depending on how the festival wants to sell their offerings. For instance, we have one client that uses a separate label specifically for their festival's travel package offerings. In Backstage, orders will display which label and which events were purchased.

**Label Page** — The label page is a term used for a part of our Purchase Path. It refers to the initial ticketing web page for a festival or event that lists all things for sale via the web at that time. This is the "page" that a viewer is taken to when clicking "Home" on any of our ticketing sites.

**Lasershark** — Lasershark is a mobile application that was developed in house to fulfill Will Call orders at the Box Office using only a handheld scanning device and is now also used for all types of fulfillment. For example, Lasershark works by first scanning a specific QR code associated with an order and then scanning the wristband's RFID chip. This links the physical wristband to the order. As it has added more functionality, Lasershark has taken the place of some of our older applications like Scan One. (This term is not to be confused with the Laser Sharks, Front Gate's softball team.)

**Layaway Plan** — A layaway plan is a way to pay for an order. It offers fans an easily managed option to pay for their ticketing purchase in multiple, planned installments. This way they can pay over a longer period instead of paying the entire amount of the order at time of purchase. Layaway plans are only offered online via our Purchase Path.

**Layaway Plan Fee** — The layaway plan fee is an upfront, non-refundable fee the customer is charged for the privilege of using a layaway plan. FGT's portion is contractually determined but many clients put their own fee on top of this. The fee falls under Handling for reporting purposes. When the Fan agrees to the terms of a Layaway Plan during the purchase process, this fee is part of what is disclosed.

**Layaway Termination Fee** — The layaway termination fee is the amount over the plan fee that will be retained if a customer defaults on their plan or voluntarily cancels their plan. FGT's portion is contractually determined but many clients put their own fee on top of this. When the Fan agrees to the terms of a Layaway Plan during the purchase process, this fee is part of what is disclosed.

**Lennd** — Lennd is a credentials management vendor that has integrated with FGT APIs for festival staff and guest list access control. Just like Fans, workers at a Festival get wristbands through the integration allowing promoters easier management of staff. A company like Lennd is hired by the festival promoter but requires a technical integration with FGT.

**License** — In the Front Gate system, the concept of a license is basically a collection of labels. Licenses contain many labels. Labels contain many events. Licenses are most commonly used when we need to be able to segregate the accounting or data access for one client or group of labels from the rest.

**Lid** — A Lid is part of our gate hardware that contains the mechanisms that read the RFID signals. Can be either mounted on a wall or on a stand as part of a Front Gate Tickets gate.

**Listing Image** — Listing image refers to the image associated with an event or item for sale that appears in the ticketing websites.

**LNE** — Live Nation Entertainment - Our parent company. See Live Nation.

**Live Nation (LNE)** — Live Nation Entertainment Inc, more commonly referred to as Live Nation or LNE, is our parent company. Live Nation is a publicly traded global live entertainment company and trades under the ticker LYV. It is headquartered in Los Angeles, CA and has multiple business segments including Concerts, Sponsorship & Advertising, Ticketing and Artist Nation. Front Gate lives in the Ticketing business segment under the management of segment leader Ticketmaster.

**Locked Seat** — When a seat is listed for resale on TM resale, it is locked. Once our system receives the message from TM Resale that the seat has been resold, the status of the ticket is changed to "Resold".

**Lyte** — Lyte is a partner in the secondary ticket business. They provide a way for fans to shop for tickets that are sold out through Front Gate but are offered on the Lyte platform by other ticket buyers for resale. For festivals that engage with Lyte services, FGT will post a link to Lyte in our Purchase Path when tickets are sold out. The integration with Lyte uses our APIs designed for Third Party Commerce partners. The benefit of using Lyte is that the client can guarantee the validity of the tickets offered for sale through that partner.

### M

**Mail Chimp** — Mail Chimp provides a marketing automation platform that helps us share email and ad campaigns with clients, customers, and other interested parties.

**Marcato** — Marcato is a credentials management vendor headquartered in Canada that has integrated with FGT for festival staff and guest list access control. Just like Fans, workers at a Festival get wristbands through an integration between Marcato and FGT allowing promoters easier management of staff. A company like Marcato is hired by the festival promoter but requires a technical integration with FGT.
